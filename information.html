INFORMATION ABOUT BILL CLINTON

Bill Clinton, the 42nd President of the United States, served two terms from 1993 to 2001. A member of the Democratic Party and a former Governor of Arkansas, his presidency was defined by a period of strong economic growth and the first federal budget surpluses in decades.

He signed major legislation such as the North American Free Trade Agreement (NAFTA) and the Welfare Reform Act. Beyond economics, his time in office saw the successful mediation of the Good Friday Agreement in Northern Ireland and intervention in the Balkan conflicts.

Despite facing a high-profile impeachment by the House of Representatives in 1998 (of which he was later acquitted by the Senate), he left office with some of the highest approval ratings of any modern president.
